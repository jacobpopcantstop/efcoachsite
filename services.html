IEP Translation & Advocacy

"I speak School District so you don't have to."

$250+ per meeting

What's Included

  • Pre-meeting review of all IEP documents
  • 30-minute prep call to understand your goals
  • Attendance at your IEP meeting (virtual or in-person)
  • Real-time "translation" of district-speak
  • Post-meeting summary and action items
  • Follow-up email support for 2 weeks

Why This Matters

IEP meetings are overwhelming. The jargon is confusing. The district has lawyers and specialists—you have... Google.

My Special Education background means I understand what accommodations actually help (vs. checkbox compliance). I'll make sure your child gets what they need, not just what's easy for the school.

Common Issues I Help With

  • Getting meaningful accommodations (not just "preferential seating")
  • Understanding evaluation results and eligibility
  • Requesting appropriate services and supports
  • Navigating disagreements with the district
  • Transitioning from IEP to 504 (or vice versa)

Note: I am not a lawyer or formal advocate. For complex legal disputes, I can recommend San Diego-area special education attorneys. My role is as a knowledgeable support person who helps you understand the process.

Frequently Asked Questions

What ages do you work with?

Most of Jacob Rozansky's students are 10-22 years old—from upper elementary through college. The sweet spot is middle school and high school, when EF demands spike but brains are still highly trainable.

Do you diagnose ADHD or learning disabilities?

No. I'm a coach, not a clinician. I work with students who have diagnoses (or suspect they might), but evaluation and diagnosis should come from a psychologist or psychiatrist.

Is this tutoring?

Not exactly. I don't teach math or write essays for students. I teach them how to plan, organize, and execute—so they can do the academic work themselves. That said, we often use real homework as practice material.

Do you work virtually or in-person?

Both. I see students in-person throughout San Diego County and virtually anywhere. The methodology works well on Zoom—especially chess and cubing, which translate easily to screen share.

How long do students typically work with you?

Most families commit to at least a semester (4 months). Executive function skills take time to build—there's no quick fix. Some students continue for years; others "graduate" after 6-12 months.

Do you take insurance?

I don't bill insurance directly. However, I can provide documentation (superbills) that some families use for HSA/FSA reimbursement or out-of-network claims. Check with your provider.

What if my child resists coaching?

Resistance is normal—especially if they've had bad tutoring experiences. That's exactly why I use games and play. Most resistant students warm up within 2-3 sessions when they realize this isn't "more school."

Can you help with college applications?

I can help with the executive function side—planning the timeline, breaking down essays, managing the overwhelm. I don't do essay editing or admissions consulting, but I know good people who do.
